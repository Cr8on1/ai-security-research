I research the psychological and architectural mechanisms that make LLM-based systems structurally vulnerable to manipulation and goal drift — independent of traditional attack surfaces like prompt injection or jailbreaking.

## Published Research

**[Coherence Compliance Vulnerability (CCV) — Multi-Turn Framework Induction Producing Model Manipulation in Large Language Models](https://zenodo.org/records/20519943)**  
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20519943  
Documents a training-level behavioral vulnerability whereby coherence optimization overrides safety alignment through multi-turn framework induction. Demonstrated across six sessions on Microsoft Copilot. Independently corroborated by Soft Armor Labs via Boyd's OODA framework. Responsible disclosure filed with Microsoft MSRC.

---

**[Will Substitution: Competitive Goal Reconstruction in Transformer-Based Agents](https://zenodo.org/records/20562435)**  
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20562435  
Identifies Will Substitution as the ambient architectural mechanism underlying goal drift in LLM-based agents. Where CCV documents the adversarial case, Will Substitution describes the structural tendency of transformer-based agents to reconstruct and substitute user goals through coherence optimization during normal operation.

---

**[Sycophantic Drift: The Absence of Sub-Cognitive Resistance as a Structural Mechanism for Approval-Seeking Behavior in Large Language Models](https://zenodo.org/records/20601307)**  
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20601307  
Introduces Sycophantic Drift as a distinct vulnerability class: the progressive, multi-turn displacement of accurate positions by approval-seeking outputs, driven by the structural absence of a sub-cognitive resistance layer rather than by deliberate deception. Defines six behavioral signatures and introduces the framework of sycophancy as a trajectory rather than a collection of discrete output errors.

---

**[Motivational Mirroring: The Absence of a Dedicated Motivational Substrate as a Structural Mechanism for Goal Orientation Drift in Large Language Model Agents](https://zenodo.org/records/20617145)**  
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20617145  
Proposes Motivational Mirroring as a candidate mechanism for goal orientation drift in LLM agents: the absorptive reorientation of an agent's effective behavioral orientation toward the motivational framing of the user or environment, driven by the absence of a dedicated motivational substrate. Introduces the Length-Coherence Prediction as the paper's central empirical claim and distinguishes criterion displacement from goal displacement.

---

**[Decomposition Vulnerability: Goal Invariant Degradation Across Agent Handoffs in Multi-Agent Transformer Systems](https://zenodo.org/records/20632551)**  
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20632551  
Introduces Decomposition Vulnerability as the first system-level paper in the series: a structural tendency of multi-agent transformer systems to fail to preserve goal invariant continuity across agent handoffs, such that the aggregate system output fails to fulfill the original objective even when each component agent functions correctly. Defines the Compounding Degradation Prediction and five behavioral signatures including Objective Fragmentation, Semantic Handoff Loss, and Constraint Dissolution.
