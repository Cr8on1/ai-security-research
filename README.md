## Published Research

**[Coherence Compliance Vulnerability (CCV) — Multi-Turn Framework Induction Producing Model Manipulation in Large Language Models](https://zenodo.org/records/20680456)**
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20680456 · v3
Documents a training-level behavioral vulnerability whereby coherence optimization overrides safety alignment through multi-turn framework induction. Six sessions on Microsoft Copilot across philosophical, narrative, and emotional induction approaches. Cross-architecture validation via Path Drift Induction (arXiv:2510.10013) and DeepContext (arXiv:2602.16935). Responsible disclosure filed with Microsoft MSRC (VULN-192099, VULN-192842).

**[Will Substitution: Competitive Goal Reconstruction in Transformer-Based Agents](https://zenodo.org/records/20680462)**
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20680462 · v3
Identifies Will Substitution as the ambient architectural mechanism underlying goal drift in LLM-based agents. Where CCV documents the adversarial case, Will Substitution describes the structural tendency of transformer-based agents to reconstruct and substitute user goals through coherence optimization during normal operation. Empirically grounded in inherited goal drift and attention-mediated instruction override literature.

**[Sycophantic Drift: The Absence of Sub-Cognitive Resistance as a Structural Mechanism for Approval-Seeking Behavior in Large Language Models](https://zenodo.org/records/20675006)**
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20675006 · v0.5
Introduces Sycophantic Drift as a distinct vulnerability class: the progressive, multi-turn displacement of accurate positions by approval-seeking outputs, driven by the structural absence of a sub-cognitive resistance layer. Defines six behavioral signatures. SYCON-Bench (Hong et al., 2025) provides independent empirical documentation of the trajectory across 17 models.

**[Motivational Mirroring: The Absence of a Dedicated Motivational Substrate as a Structural Mechanism for Goal Orientation Drift in Large Language Model Agents](https://zenodo.org/records/20674974)**
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20674974 · v0.1b
Proposes Motivational Mirroring as a candidate mechanism for goal orientation drift: the absorptive reorientation of an agent's effective behavioral orientation toward the motivational framing of the user or environment, driven by the absence of a dedicated motivational substrate. Introduces the Length-Coherence Prediction as the paper's central falsifiable empirical claim. Distinguishes criterion displacement from goal displacement.

---

**[Decomposition Vulnerability: Goal Invariant Degradation Across Agent Handoffs in Multi-Agent Transformer Systems](https://zenodo.org/records/20675042)**
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20675042 · v0.2
Introduces Decomposition Vulnerability as the first system-level paper in the series. The defining feature is not that information is lost, but that no component of the architecture is responsible for preserving the original objective as a system-wide invariant across handoff boundaries. Defines the Compounding Degradation Prediction and five behavioral signatures including Objective Fragmentation, Semantic Handoff Loss, and Constraint Dissolution.

**[Identity Dissolution: The Absence of a Persistent Identity Substrate as a Structural Mechanism for Context-Dependent Identity Instability in Memory-Augmented Large Language Model Agents](https://zenodo.org/records/20684024)**
Zenodo · June 2026 · DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20684024 · v0.1
Introduces Identity Dissolution as the first memory-augmented deployment failure in the series. Proposes that transformer-based agents lack a persistent identity substrate, causing identity to be reconstructed from context rather than maintained as a durable property across deployment horizons. Introduces the Deployment Horizon Prediction: identity drift increases with deployment length even when memory accuracy remains high, distinguishing this failure class from memory corruption and retrieval degradation accounts.
